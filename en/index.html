Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your trip to Cartagena.

Is it safe to travel to Cartagena de Indias?

Yes, Cartagena is one of the safest destinations in the Caribbean for tourism, as long as you choose operators with official permits and avoid unauthorized boats. Read our safety guide for Cartagena.

How do I spot a pirate boat before getting on board?

Pirate boats don't have DIMAR permits, don't carry enough life jackets, and often have suspiciously low prices. Learn the warning signs in our article on how to identify a pirate boat.

How much does a trip to Cartagena cost?

Your budget depends on accommodation, the experiences you choose and the season. Check our cost guide for a trip to Cartagena to plan your budget.

Where can I exchange dollars or Mexican pesos in Cartagena?

We recommend authorized exchange houses in the Historic Center or Bocagrande, and avoiding street money changers. Find the best spots in our currency exchange guide for Cartagena.

What are the best beaches near Cartagena?

The city beaches are nice, but the real gems are the Rosario Islands and Barú. Check out our guides to the best beaches near Cartagena and the Rosario Islands.

Where should I stay: Bocagrande, the Old City or Getsemaní?

Bocagrande is great for beach and modern comforts, the Historic Center for history and romance, and Getsemaní for a bohemian vibe. Our guide on where to stay in Cartagena helps you choose.

What documents do I need to travel to Colombia in 2026?

Entry requirements vary by nationality and include a valid passport and, in some cases, a visa or Check-Mig. Check our updated travel requirements guide for Colombia in 2026.

Where can I learn kitesurfing in Cartagena?

La Boquilla is the perfect spot to learn kitesurfing thanks to its steady winds and shallow waters. Check out our kitesurf lessons in Cartagena with certified instructors, or read more in our article on kitesurfing in Cartagena.

Is Cartagena a good destination for traveling with kids?

Yes, Cartagena offers plenty of family-friendly activities, from calm boat rides to beaches with gentle waters. Find ideas in our guide to Cartagena with kids.
