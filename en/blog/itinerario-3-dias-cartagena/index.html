3-Day Itinerary for Cartagena: What to Do Day by Day

By Nohemi 9 min read

Three days is the perfect amount of time to discover Cartagena's three faces: its colonial, historic side, its island and ocean side, and its deep-Caribbean side with beaches and wind sports. This itinerary is designed so you don't waste time figuring things out on the fly and can make the most of every day. Of course, you can adapt it to your own taste and the day's weather (check our guide on the best time to visit Cartagena if you're still choosing your dates).

Day 1: Historic Center, the Walled City and Getsemaní

The first day is for walking. Start the morning exploring the cobblestone streets of the Historic Center: the Cathedral, the Palace of the Inquisition, the squares of Santo Domingo, Bolívar and San Diego, and of course the city walls that give Cartagena its nickname, "the Little Stone Corral" (if you're curious about where that name comes from, read our article on what Cartagena used to be called).

At midday, have lunch at one of the restaurants in the Center or cross over to Getsemaní, the bohemian neighborhood full of murals, music, and lively squares. In the afternoon, explore Getsemaní's narrow streets and end the day walking along the wall toward the Santo Domingo Bastion or Plaza de la Trinidad to watch the sunset — one of the city's most iconic experiences (we cover more in our guide to the best sunsets in Cartagena).

Tip: Wear comfortable shoes — the streets of the Center and Getsemaní are stone and cobblestone, and you'll be walking a lot all day.

Day 2: Full-day boat trip to the Rosario Islands

Set aside your second day to head out to sea. A boat trip to the Rosario Islands is the classic way to experience the turquoise waters of the Colombian Caribbean: snorkeling on coral reefs, stops at white-sand beaches and, depending on the plan, a fresh fish lunch by the sea. You can also visit the Aquarium or combine the islands with a stop in Barú.

This is the day to book ahead, since boats usually depart early in the morning from the city's docks. In our guide to the best Rosario Islands we break down the differences between each island so you can choose the one that fits you best, and on our experiences page you'll find shared and private boat options.

Day 3: La Boquilla, beach and kitesurfing

The third day is for discovering the "local" Caribbean: La Boquilla, a fishing village north of the city with long beaches, mangroves and the best wind for water sports. If you've never tried kitesurfing, this is your chance — a first lesson of a couple of hours gives you a real feel for it, with all equipment included and a certified instructor (read more in our guide to kitesurfing in Cartagena or check out our kitesurf lessons in La Boquilla).

If you'd rather take it slow, simply enjoy the beach, try the food at the seaside restaurants, and get a taste of the area's Afro-Caribbean culture (we recommend our article on Caribbean cuisines to better understand what you're about to eat).

Where to stay to make this itinerary easier

The neighborhood where you stay affects how much time you spend getting between activities. Bocagrande and the Historic Center/Getsemaní are the most practical choices for combining walking, nightlife and proximity to the boat docks. If your priority is La Boquilla and kitesurfing, staying near that area can save you transfer time. Check our full guide on where to stay in Cartagena to decide based on your itinerary.

Getting around between areas

Within the Historic Center and Getsemaní, walking is best — the streets are narrow and many are pedestrian-only. To get to Bocagrande, the boat docks, or La Boquilla, the most practical option is a taxi or private transport arranged with your accommodation or your experience operator, especially if you're carrying beach or kitesurf gear.

Have more than 3 days?

If you have more time, you can add a full day of progressive kitesurf lessons, a food tour through the Center, a longer beach day in Barú, or explore family-friendly options (check our guide to Cartagena with kids). You can also browse our list of things to do in Cartagena to customize your itinerary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 3 days enough to see Cartagena?

Yes, for the essentials: the Historic Center, a day of islands by boat, and a day of beach or kitesurfing in La Boquilla. With more time, you can go deeper into each area.

How do I get around between areas?

On foot within the Center and Getsemaní; by taxi or private transport to Bocagrande, the docks, or La Boquilla.

What shouldn't I miss?

A sunset walk along the city walls, a day of island-hopping by boat with snorkeling, and an afternoon in La Boquilla.
