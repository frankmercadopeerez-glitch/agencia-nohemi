How Much Does a Trip to Cartagena Cost? Real Budget for 2025-2026

By Nohemi 12 min read

One of the most common questions before traveling to Cartagena is how much money to bring. The answer depends a lot on what kind of traveler you are and what you expect from your experience. This budget guide uses real 2025-2026 prices, not catalog estimates.

Flights: how much to pay and when to buy

Cartagena's Rafael Núñez Airport (CTG) receives direct flights from Bogotá, Medellín, Cali, Barranquilla, and a few cities in the United States and Panama.

  • Bogotá to Cartagena: $120,000 – $400,000 COP one-way depending on the airline and how far ahead you book. Buy 4-8 weeks in advance for the best prices.
  • Medellín to Cartagena: $100,000 – $350,000 COP one-way.
  • Miami to Cartagena (direct): USD 200 – USD 500 one-way. Spirit and Copa have direct routes.
  • New York via Bogotá or Panama: USD 300 – USD 700 one-way depending on layover and season.

Best time for cheap flights: Buying during low season (May-June, September-October) to travel during high season (December-January) can save you up to 40%.

Accommodation: options for every budget

Backpacker / Low budget: Hostels in Getsemaní have shared dorm beds from $40,000 – $70,000 COP per night. Private room in a hostel: $100,000 – $180,000 COP.

Mid-range budget: 3-star hotel in Bocagrande or Getsemaní: $200,000 – $350,000 COP per night. Apartment or Airbnb in the Centro Histórico for groups: $300,000 – $600,000 COP total.

High-end / luxury: Boutique hotels in the Centro Histórico or oceanfront in Castillogrande: $500,000 – $2,000,000 COP per night. During the December-January season, prices can be 50-100% higher.

Getting around Cartagena

  • Metered taxi: $8,000 – $15,000 COP within the tourist area. Without a meter, negotiate before getting in.
  • Uber / InDriver: $6,000 – $12,000 COP for the same routes. More predictable and with a trip record.
  • Mototaxi: $5,000 – $8,000 COP for short rides. Cheap, but somewhat risky if the driver doesn't know the route well.
  • Urban bus: $2,500 COP per ride. The cheapest option, but not recommended for tourists with luggage.

Food: where costs vary the most

Food is where you can save the most or spend the most in Cartagena, depending on where you choose to eat:

  • Local market / corrientazo: $15,000 – $35,000 COP per person (includes soup, main course, juice, and dessert).
  • Neighborhood restaurant: $30,000 – $60,000 COP per person.
  • Tourist restaurant in the Centro Histórico: $70,000 – $150,000 COP per person.
  • Fine dining restaurant: $150,000 – $300,000 COP per person.

Tours and experiences: real prices

Here are the prices for the main experiences in Cartagena:

  • Rosario Islands day trip (private boat + snorkel + lunch): See Nohemi experiences for current pricing.
  • Bay sunset tour: $120,000 – $180,000 COP per person.
  • Kitesurf course (beginner level, 9 hours): From $750,000 COP.
  • San Felipe Castle: $22,000 COP for foreigners, $6,500 COP for Colombians.
  • Private boat rental: $3,200,000 – $3,900,000 COP for a full day (for the whole group).

Daily budget by traveler profile

Backpacker: $180,000 – $250,000 COP/day (hostel, local food, public transportation, one budget tour every 2-3 days).

Mid-range traveler: $400,000 – $700,000 COP/day (3-star hotel, varied restaurants, occasional tours).

Luxury trip: $1,000,000 – $3,000,000 COP/day per person (boutique hotel, premium restaurants, private boat).

Frequently asked questions

Is Cartagena expensive compared to the rest of Colombia?

It's one of the most expensive destinations in the country, especially for accommodation. Local food is affordable; the main difference is in hotels and tours.

How much does a private boat cost split among a group?

Between $3.2M and $3.9M COP for the day. Split among 8-10 people: $320,000-$490,000 COP per person for the whole day.

Can I travel on a tight budget?

Yes. With $150,000-$200,000 COP per day (hostel + local food) you'll have a real experience, though a more modest one.
