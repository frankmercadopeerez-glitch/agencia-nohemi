Wing Foil vs Kitesurf in La Boquilla: Which to Choose?

By Nohemi 8 min read

La Boquilla, north of Cartagena, has become one of the best spots in the Caribbean for wind sports. If you're planning your trip and wondering whether to try kitesurfing or wing foiling, you're not alone — every year more travelers arrive with the same question. Here are the key differences so you can choose the experience that best fits your level, your available time, and the feeling you're looking for on the water.

What is kitesurfing?

Kitesurfing combines a large kite (controlled with a bar and lines) with a board similar to a wakeboard or surfboard. The kite generates the pull that propels you across the water, and with practice it allows jumps, turns, and high-speed runs. It's a very complete sport that combines strength, technique, and reading the wind.

What is wing foiling?

Wing foiling is newer: it uses an inflatable "wing" that you hold with your hands (no lines or mandatory harness) over a board with a hydrodynamic foil underneath. The foil "lifts" the board out of the water at low speed, creating a smooth, silent glide — almost like flying just above the surface.

Differences in the learning curve

Kitesurfing first requires learning to control the kite from the beach (called "kite flying") before getting on the board, and then coordinating both at the same time. It usually takes several sessions before you can ride independently for your first few meters.

Wing foiling, on the other hand, has no lines to manage: the wing is held directly with your hands, which reduces that initial feeling of "something that can get out of control." However, balancing on the foil — which reacts very sensitively — is a different challenge that also takes practice.

Tip: if you've never tried a board sport powered by wind, a couple-hour introductory lesson with a certified instructor will help you feel the differences firsthand before deciding which one to focus on.

Wind conditions in La Boquilla

Kitesurfing generally makes the most of medium-to-strong, steady wind, which is common in La Boquilla for much of the year (check our guide on the best time to visit Cartagena to plan around the wind). Wing foiling, thanks to the lift generated by the foil, can be practiced even in somewhat lighter wind, which widens the practice windows throughout the day.

Equipment and costs

Both sports require specialized equipment: a kite, bar, and board for kitesurfing; a wing and a foil board for wing foiling. If you're just starting out, you don't need to buy anything — in our lessons in La Boquilla all equipment is included, so you can try it before investing in your own gear.

Which is better for beginners?

There's no single answer — it depends on your profile:

  • If you're drawn to speed, jumps, and a sport with more "adrenaline" from the start: kitesurfing tends to be more thrilling once you get past the initial learning curve.
  • If you're looking for a smoother, quieter feeling and want to take advantage of lighter-wind days too: wing foiling can feel more accessible.
  • If you're not sure: many people try kitesurfing first since it's more well-known, then move on to wing foiling as a second discipline (or vice versa).

Our recommendation

If it's your first time with wind sports, we recommend starting with a kitesurf lesson — it's the discipline with the most instructors, equipment, and track record in La Boquilla (read more in our guide to kitesurfing in Cartagena). If you already have experience with a board sport (surfing, snowboarding, wakeboarding) and want to try something different, wing foiling can be an excellent entry point into foil sports.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is wing foil or kitesurf harder to learn?

Kitesurfing has a longer initial learning curve due to kite handling. Wing foiling feels more intuitive at first, though balancing on the foil has its own challenge.

How much wind do I need for each?

Kitesurfing makes the most of medium-to-strong, steady wind. Wing foiling can be practiced in lighter wind thanks to the foil.

Can I learn both in La Boquilla?

Yes, La Boquilla has favorable conditions and certified instructors for both disciplines for most of the year.
