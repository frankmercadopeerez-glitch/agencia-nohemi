Bocagrande, Old Town or Getsemaní: Where to Stay in Cartagena?

By Nohemi 10 min read

One of the first decisions you'll make when planning your trip to Cartagena is where to stay, and it can make a big difference in your experience. The city has neighborhoods with very different personalities: modern beachfront towers, colonial streets with centuries of history, or bohemian areas full of art and nightlife. In this guide we compare the three most popular areas among visitors — Bocagrande, the Old Town (Centro Histórico) and Getsemaní — and give you our recommendation based on your type of trip.

Bocagrande: beach, modern comfort and convenience

Bocagrande is Cartagena's modern hotel district: a narrow peninsula lined with high-rise buildings facing the Caribbean Sea. It's the closest thing to a "classic" beach destination, with sand just steps from the hotels, international restaurants, supermarkets, ATMs and shopping centers.

  • Best for: families, travelers who prioritize the beach and convenience, and anyone who prefers walking on wide, well-lit sidewalks.
  • Vibe: modern, orderly and very touristy, similar in style to resort areas in other Caribbean destinations.
  • Distances: about 15-20 minutes by taxi from the Old Town and the airport.

Old Town (Centro Histórico): colonial magic inside the walls

The Centro Histórico is the heart of Cartagena: cobblestone streets, flower-covered balconies, colorful plazas and buildings dating back to colonial times, all surrounded by the walls that protected the city for centuries. Staying here means having the walled city literally at your doorstep.

  • Best for: travelers who want to experience the historic atmosphere day and night, honeymooners, and anyone who prioritizes being close to restaurants, bars and cultural attractions.
  • Vibe: romantic, photogenic and lively, especially at sunset when the streets fill with music.
  • Keep in mind: boutique hotels inside the walls tend to have higher rates, and the streets are pedestrian or restricted to traffic (rolling suitcases can be tricky on the cobblestones).

Getsemaní: street art, nightlife and budget-friendly options

Right next to the Old Town, separated by a small park, Getsemaní is a neighborhood that went from a working-class area to one of Cartagena's most photographed destinations thanks to its colorful murals, streets lined with umbrellas and flags, and a wide range of food and nightlife options.

  • Best for: younger travelers, backpackers, couples and anyone looking for better value without straying far from the center.
  • Vibe: bohemian, artistic and lively, with a mix of hostels, boutique hotels and restaurants across every price range.
  • Keep in mind: it's very lively at night, so if you want a quiet place to sleep, choose accommodation away from the streets with the most bars.

To discover the best places to eat and go out in this area, check out our Cartagena food guide.

Other areas worth considering: Manga, Castillogrande and La Boquilla

If you're looking for something different, these areas are also good options:

  • Manga: a quiet residential neighborhood with republican-style houses and a more local atmosphere. It's well connected by taxi to both the Old Town and the airport.
  • Castillogrande: right next to Bocagrande, with a quieter residential feel but still close to the beach and amenities.
  • La Boquilla: north of the city, near the mangroves, with direct access to the docks where many boat tours to the Rosario Islands depart. A good option if your main plan is to spend time on the water.

What if your main plan is to get out on the water?

If the main reason for your trip is enjoying the Caribbean — white sand beaches, snorkeling and sunsets on a boat — any of these neighborhoods works well as a base, since most tours depart from docks near Bocagrande, Manga or La Boquilla. To explore your beach and island options, check out our guide to the best Rosario Islands.

Our recommendation based on your trip

Traveling with family or kids: Bocagrande, for its urban beach, amenities and wide sidewalks.
First time visiting and want the colonial magic: the Old Town, to have everything within steps.
Looking for great atmosphere, art and more flexible prices: Getsemaní, without straying from the center.
Your priority is the sea and boat tours: any area works, but Bocagrande and La Boquilla tend to be closest to the docks.

Safety by neighborhood

Bocagrande, the Old Town, Manga and Castillogrande are areas with a strong police presence and are considered safe for visitors, both day and night on their main streets. Getsemaní is also safe, though it's wise to apply the same basic precautions you would in any tourist city. For more details, read our full Cartagena safety guide.

Tip: no matter which neighborhood you choose, use authorized taxis or ride-hailing apps to get around at night, and avoid walking alone on poorly lit streets.

Frequently asked questions

What is the safest neighborhood to stay in Cartagena?

Bocagrande, the Old Town and Manga are tourist areas with a strong police presence and are considered safe for visitors. Getsemaní is also safe during the day and night on its main streets.

Which area is best for traveling with kids?

Bocagrande tends to be the favorite for families thanks to its urban beach, wide sidewalks, nearby supermarkets and hotels with pools.

Which neighborhood is closest to the airport?

Bocagrande and the Old Town are about 15-20 minutes by taxi from Rafael Núñez International Airport.
