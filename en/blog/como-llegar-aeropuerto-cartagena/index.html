How to Get from the Airport to Your Hotel in Cartagena

By Nohemi 7 min read

One of Cartagena's great advantages is that its airport, Rafael Núñez (code CTG), is just minutes away from the city's most visited areas. Still, arriving in a new country or city always raises questions: should I take a taxi? Is there private transport? Is it safe? Here we explain all the options so your arrival is smooth from the very first minute.

Rafael Núñez Airport: location and distances

The airport is located in the Crespo neighborhood, very close to the northern part of the city and a short distance from the Historic Center, Getsemaní, and Bocagrande. This proximity is a big advantage — you don't need long transfers to start enjoying your trip.

  • To the Historic Center / Getsemaní: approximately 15-20 minutes.
  • To Bocagrande: approximately 15-25 minutes, depending on traffic.
  • To La Boquilla: approximately 20-35 minutes.
  • To Manzanillo del Mar or areas further north: 30-45 minutes.

Option 1: Official airport taxi

The most common way to get to your hotel is the official airport taxi. As you exit the baggage area, you'll find an authorized point where airport staff assign you a vehicle and tell you the fare based on your destination, following a regulated price table. Pay at that point or directly to the driver as instructed, and keep the receipt if you're given one.

Important: avoid agreeing on fares with people who approach you offering transport before you reach the official taxi point. Always use the authorized counter inside the terminal.

Option 2: Private transport or pre-arranged transfer

If you'd rather not worry about anything when you land, you can arrange a private transfer with your hotel or with us before your flight. A driver waits for you in the arrivals area with a sign showing your name, helps with your luggage, and takes you straight to your accommodation. It's the most comfortable option, especially if you're arriving at night, traveling with kids, or carrying a lot of luggage.

Tip: if you're booking several experiences with us (boat tours, kitesurfing, etc.), ask us about an airport transfer — we can often coordinate it as part of your plan.

Option 3: Ride-hailing apps

Apps like Uber or InDriver operate in Cartagena, although their use from the airport can be limited or subject to local restrictions in the pickup area. If you decide to use them, keep in mind you may need to walk to a point outside the terminal's immediate perimeter to meet your driver.

Exchange some money when you arrive

Before leaving the airport, it's a good idea to have some Colombian pesos in cash for the taxi, a tip, or a drink while you settle in. If you didn't get a chance to exchange money before your trip, check our guide on how to exchange currency in Cartagena to do it safely and at the best rate.

What if my flight lands at night?

Rafael Núñez Airport receives flights until late, and the official taxi point operates during all hours of operation. Even so, if you arrive in the early morning or very late, a pre-arranged private transfer is especially recommended — you won't have to wait in line, and your driver will be waiting for you with your name.

First steps once you reach your hotel

Once you're at your hotel, take a moment to check our guide on what to do in Cartagena to plan your first days, or if you want to organize your whole visit, take a look at our 3-day itinerary for Cartagena. And if you have questions about safety in the city, we recommend our guide to safety in Cartagena.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the trip from the airport to downtown take?

Between 15 and 25 minutes under normal traffic conditions. To La Boquilla it can take 20 to 35 minutes.

Is it safe to take a taxi at the airport?

Yes, as long as you use the official taxi point inside the terminal and avoid offers from people outside that point.

How much does a taxi from the airport to a hotel cost?

It depends on the destination area and follows an official rate table. Going to the Center or Bocagrande is usually cheaper than going to La Boquilla.
