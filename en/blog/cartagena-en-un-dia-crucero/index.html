Cartagena Cruise Port Guide: What to Do in One Day

By Nohemi 9 min read

Cartagena is one of the favorite stops for cruise ships sailing the Caribbean, and for good reason: in just a few hours you can explore a 500-year-old walled city, try authentic Caribbean food, and if time allows, hop on a boat to see the city from the water. This guide is built for cruise passengers who want to make the most of their day ashore without risking missing their ship's departure.

From the port to the Old Town

Cartagena's cruise terminal is located near the Centro Histórico (Old Town), about a 10-15 minute taxi ride depending on traffic and which pier your ship docks at. Most cruise lines offer organized transport to the city walls, but you can also take an authorized taxi directly from the terminal.

Important: note your ship's exact departure time and plan to be back at the port at least 60-90 minutes early, accounting for afternoon traffic in Cartagena.

Itineraries based on your hours in port

If you have 4-5 hours

Focus on the walled Old Town: walk through Plaza de los Coches, Plaza Santo Domingo and Plaza de la Aduana, walk along the city walls for Caribbean Sea views, and finish with a coffee or tropical juice on one of the plazas. This is plenty of time for a relaxed, complete walking experience.

If you have 6-7 hours

Beyond the Old Town, cross into Getsemaní to see its street art and bohemian atmosphere, or visit the San Felipe de Barajas Castle, the largest fortress built by the Spanish in the Americas.

If you have 8 hours or more

With a full day in port, you can combine the Old Town with a short boat ride on the bay to see the walled city from the sea and enjoy the ocean air — the most memorable way to say goodbye to Cartagena before heading back to your ship.

Plan ahead: if you want to combine your stop with a boat outing, message us on WhatsApp before your trip with your exact departure time and we'll put together a plan that fits your schedule.

What to see and where to eat near the Old Town

  • The City Walls: walk along them at sunset (if your schedule allows) for a spectacular view of the city and the sea.
  • Plaza Santo Domingo: surrounded by restaurants and home to the famous "Gertrudis" sculpture by Fernando Botero.
  • Quick, authentic food: try an arepa de huevo or a corozo juice at a stand in the Old Town — the fastest way to taste local flavors without sitting down for a long lunch.
  • For a fuller food experience (if you have time), check our guide to Cartagena's food scene.

Safety tips for cruise passengers

The Old Town and surrounding areas are busy, generally safe tourist zones during the day. Still, keep your belongings close, avoid flashing expensive cameras unnecessarily, and use only authorized taxis or your cruise line's organized transport to get back to the port. For more detail, read our full Cartagena safety guide.

What to avoid if your time is limited

  • Don't wander too far from the Old Town without carefully calculating return times; traffic in Cartagena can be unpredictable.
  • Avoid last-minute souvenir shopping near the pier at inflated prices; you'll always find better prices and selection in the Old Town.
  • Don't leave currency exchange for the last minute: exchange some cash as soon as you disembark.

Frequently asked questions

How far is the port from the Old Town?

About 10-15 minutes by taxi depending on traffic and which pier you dock at.

Can I do a boat tour on a cruise day?

Yes, if you have 8 hours or more in port. Message us before your trip so we can plan something that fits your departure time.

What currency do I need?

The Colombian peso (COP). Many Old Town businesses accept dollars or cards, but it helps to carry some pesos in cash for taxis and small purchases.
