Barú, Cartagena

The white-sand peninsula with turquoise waters less than an hour from Cartagena de Indias.

Home / Experiences / Barú

Barú is one of the most accessible gems of the Colombian Caribbean. Technically a peninsula connected to the mainland by land since 2004 (though many still call it an "island"), it sits southwest of Cartagena about 25 km away. It's home to some of Colombia's most beautiful beaches: white sand, turquoise water and palm trees straight out of a postcard.

Unlike the Rosario Islands, Barú can be reached overland, making it especially convenient for families, people prone to seasickness, or anyone who prefers to avoid an open-water crossing by boat.

Barú's Beaches

Playa Blanca — The most famous

Colombia's most photographed beach and, for many visitors, its most beautiful. Powder-white sand, crystal-clear water in multiple shades of blue and turquoise, and a lively atmosphere with coconut vendors, crafts and seafood. It's split between a public zone (free access but crowded) and several beach clubs like Puerto Medallo, which offer controlled access with food and pool included.

Polo Beach — The peaceful one

On the other side of the peninsula, Polo Beach is the complete opposite of Playa Blanca: fewer tourists, calmer water, more intimate atmosphere. The perfect choice for those who want a beach day without crowds. Access is through the Polo Beach Club complex.

Things to Do in Barú

  • 🏖️ Beach club day at Playa Blanca (Puerto Medallo) — lunch and pool included.
  • 🌊 Quiet day at Polo Beach — Barú's most relaxed shore.
  • 🦩 Colombia's National Aviary — 160 bird species including flamingos, toucans and macaws.
  • 🐾 Raccoons and snorkeling — combined with a stop at the Rosario Islands.
  • 📸 Photography — Barú's water has colors that shift uniquely with the light.

How to Get to Barú from Cartagena

There are two ways to get there:

  • By land: bus from Cartagena's market or private transport via the Pasacaballos road. The most comfortable option — no seasickness risk, air-conditioned vehicle. About 45–60 minutes. This is how our tours go.
  • By sea: boat from Muelle de los Pegasos (~30 min). Faster but exposed to swells. Available combined with Rosario Islands tours.

Nohemi's tip: if you're traveling with children or anyone who gets seasick, choose the land transport option. The road trip is equally comfortable and eliminates the open-water crossing entirely. Our land tours include pickup at your hotel or an agreed point.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is Barú and how do you get there from Cartagena?

Barú is a peninsula-island about 25 km southwest of Cartagena. You can get there by land or by boat. Our tours go by land — more comfortable for families and no seasickness.

What's the difference between Playa Blanca and Polo Beach?

Playa Blanca is Colombia's most famous beach — busy and lively. Polo Beach is quieter and more exclusive. Both have crystal-clear water and white sand but very different vibes.

What is Colombia's National Aviary in Barú?

The largest bird park in Colombia — over 900 birds of 160 species, including flamingos, toucans and macaws. Combinable with a Polo Beach day.

Can I go to Barú without a tour?

Yes, by bus from Cartagena's market. But a tour includes door-to-door transport, beach club access with facilities and food — much more comfortable and stress-free.
